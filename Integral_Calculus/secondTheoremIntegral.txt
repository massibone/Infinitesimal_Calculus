In essence, the Second Fundamental Theorem of Calculus provides a method to evaluate definite integrals by finding antiderivatives and evaluating them at the endpoints. It also establishes the connection between differentiation and integration, stating that integration undoes differentiation and vice versa.

The theorem plays a crucial role in calculus as it allows us to calculate definite integrals more easily and provides a powerful tool for solving a variety of problems in mathematics, physics, and engineering.
